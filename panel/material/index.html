Material

During some projects I assembled some data or made some movies which might be interesting to others.

  • I created the Monte-Carlo radiative transfer code tlac (first used & described in arXiv:1406.6709 ) which I am happy to share.
  • In Modeling 237 Lyman-a spectra of the MUSE-Wide survey I fitted shell-models to the publicly available MUSE spectra of the “Wide” survey. The fits can be found here.
  • Together with Mark Dijkstra, Mike McCourt & Peng Oh we showed in From Mirrors to Windows: Lyman-alpha Radiative Transfer in a Very Clumpy Medium (easy read) and Resonant line transfer in a fog: Using Lyman-alpha to probe tiny structures in atomic gas (more technical) that Lya propagation in a “fog” of small clouds resembles the radiative transfer process in a homogeneous medium. I uploaded some animations illustrating the different escape mechanisms in a (multiphase) medium.
  • In arXiv:1806.02728 we re-visited the “cloud-crushing” problem and identified a survival criterion for cold gas in a hot wind. I uploaded some videos of the simulations.
